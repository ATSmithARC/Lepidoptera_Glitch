Lepidoptera

A Computational Framework for Animal Aided Design


Lepidoptera is currently under development as part of an ongoing master's project at the Royal Danish Academy's Computation in Architecture Program. The project itself called PLANimals, uses Lepidoptera as the project's primary web platform.

The motivation for the project is simple:
The way we design and build cities, and the buildings in them impacts local and global biodiversity in profound ways. As we begin to better understand these impacts, there is a growing need for accessible platforms that facilitate a cross disciplinary and collaborative approach to quanifty and reduce those impacts. Lepidoptera is a work-in-progress, and experimental platform where scientists, architects, urban planners, and citizens can gather, contribute, communicate, and analyze structured geospatial ecological data as it relates to biodiversity.

Biodiversity loss is a growing long-term threat to humanity.

The Living Planet Index tracks global biodiversity.

The LPI trends differently for each continent.

Understanding our Ecological Footprint is essential to understanding the human impacts on biodiversity.

Specifically, 'Ecological Footprint by Land Use' is cited by the WWF as an essential metric for identifying our impact on biodiversity decline.

It is an estimate that that our cities and roads (and their embodied carbon) contribute about 30% to the global Ecological Footprint.

Cities impact biodiversity directly through land development, and indirectly through their material consumption and material transportation.

Architecture is adressing the direct impacts through a greater focus on "Ecological Design" principals and the indirect impacts though LCA.

So why, given our focus on ecological design, do we lack widely distributed tools for designing with (and for) ecologies.

This is the Research Question PLANimals, and Lepidoptera are trying to answer.

If given the correct information architects, scientists and citizens can make informed observations and design decisions.

The framework and platform proposed here is inspired by, and aims to aid various steps in the Animal Aided Design Methodology.

It is a focus of PLANimals to make the framework accessible for multiple audiences. Hence the focus on a web platform and Citizen Science.

The statistical model is a location specific, multi-scalar, structured collection of geo-spaital ecological information.
